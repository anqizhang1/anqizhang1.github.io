About 关于

On service automation, customer privacy, and the interpersonal boundaries hospitality has long taken for granted.

I am a PhD candidate in Information Systems at the J. Mack Robinson College of Business, Georgia State University. My current work investigates how the deployment of service robots alters customers' psychological state and behavior, with particular attention to spatial privacy, data privacy, and the boundaries that govern professional intimacy.

My dissertation triangulates a randomized field experiment in a hotel, a multi-stage psychometric scale development, and a controlled laboratory experiment. Together, these studies examine when the same service behavior, performed by a robot or by a human, produces fundamentally different perceptions of privacy, comfort, and quality.

Methodologically, I am drawn to mixed designs that pair field-based behavioral experiments with controlled lab experiments. I am on the academic job market for the 2026 to 2027 cycle.

Teaching 教学

On apprenticing students into the craft of careful empirical and critical reasoning.

师

传道 · 授业 · 解惑

My teaching draws on the three tenets articulated by Han Yu (768 to 824 CE) in his essay "Discourse on Teachers": to transmit the Way, to impart expertise, and to resolve doubts. I treat each tenet as a distinct posture toward the student. The first asks me to expose students to wider habits of attention, especially the discipline of asking what would have to be observed for an estimate to be wrong. The second asks me to serve as ferryman, accompanying students from familiar shores to those just beyond their reach, and at times only pointing across rather than carrying them over. The third asks me to refuse presupposition, to meet each question at the place curiosity actually begins.

I learned this posture from my mother, a teacher of more than thirty years, whose quiet delight at her students' progress remains the standard against which I measure my own.
